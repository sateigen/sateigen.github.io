My Portfolio

Below are a few of the projects that I completed during my twelve weeks at The Iron Yard.

eduFactor

eduFactor provides students with a safe learning environment to practice answering the various types of questions they will encounter on End of Year Assessments. By creating a step-by-step tutorial option, students are instructed as to how they will need to interact with the questions they face.

Flix

As my first Django project, Flix taught me quite a bit. Along with applying my Python skills to learning the Django framework, I was also able to manipulate a large database. The application allows users to view movies that have been rated, register, login, and logout. Once logged in, users can rate movies and get reccomendations of movies to watch.

Road Rage

his was a pair programming project that sought to determine the optimal speed limit for a 1km stretch of road. We utilized object-oriented programming and dove into the concept of simulations. Using matplotlib, we were able to graphically display the data that we recorded.

A Little Bit About Me

Hi! My name is Shanon Teigen and I live in Richmond, Virginia. I have recently finished a three-month Python coding bootcamp at The Iron Yard in Durham, North Carolina. Prior to attending The Iron Yard, I was a Title-I Math Specialist at an elementary school in Chesterfield, Virginia. It was during my last year teaching that I was introduced to coding and saw the impact software developers make in classrooms and decided I wanted to be a part of that. While I am not studying or coding, I enjoy riding my horses, painting, and volunteering in schools in my area.
